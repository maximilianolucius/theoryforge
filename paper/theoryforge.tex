\begin{abstract}
Autonomous systems now emit plausible research-level mathematics, but emitting a
statement is not discovery: novelty, truth, utility, and nontriviality are
separate axes. We present \tf{}, the \emph{Discovery Plane} of the \engine{}:
an operator-guided architecture whose task is not to prove a supplied theorem
but to \emph{form theory} --- to synthesize definitions, invariants,
conjectures, reductions, counterexamples, barriers, and research programs whose
value survives \emph{independent} verification. \tf{} generates typed
\emph{Mathematical Cells} through a library of thirteen discovery operators
(e.g.\ \op{SYNTHESIZE\_DEFINITION}, \op{COUNTEREXAMPLE\_FIRST},
\op{MINIMAL\_MISSING\_LEMMA}, \op{BARRIER\_SEARCH}), records their genealogy on
an immutable event log, and --- by construction, not by policy --- holds
\emph{no promotion authority}: only an independent Congress may promote a claim.
It reuses three existing substrates: \mirador{} (representation and
content-addressed identity), \pc{} (dependency-aware retrieval into hashed
Evidence Bundles), and a \cb{}-compatible reviewer interface. We define \tb{},
a four-tier evaluation ladder (synthetic worlds, retrospective rediscovery,
expert-authored unseen tasks, and a governed open frontier), a preregistered
baseline/ablation protocol, and a five-stage novelty--verification gauntlet in
which counterexample search precedes expensive proof promotion. The integrated
case study is Bang's affine plank conjecture, using a frozen, hash-pinned corpus
of a submission-ready campaign.

\medskip
\noindent\dbend~\textbf{Status.} This is a \emph{design and protocol checkpoint}.
The architecture, operator specifications, benchmark design, and evaluation
protocol are written and grounded in the existing codebases; \textbf{no
discovery experiment has been run, and no empirical result is reported.}
Sections whose content depends on frozen-then-executed experiments are marked
\dbend~\textbf{TO COMPLETE} and contain no fabricated numbers.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

A model that prints an unfamiliar-looking statement has not made a discovery.
Discovery requires that the statement be \emph{true} (or that its falsity be a
useful counterexample), \emph{novel}, \emph{nontrivial}, and \emph{useful} to
subsequent work --- four axes that must be judged separately, and none of which
is established by fluency. This paper concerns the machinery that produces
candidate mathematics under exactly that discipline.

\tf{} is the \emph{Discovery Plane} of the \engine{}~\cite{lucius2026engine}, a
governed architecture that separates two institutions: a Discovery Plane that
\emph{proposes} (and may be exploratory, redundant, or speculative) and a Trust
Plane that alone may \emph{promote} a claim into load-bearing knowledge. In the
engine's prior specification the Discovery Plane was described but not built (its
maturity code is ``conceptual'')~\ev{evidenced: Bourbaki \texttt{audits/CLAIM\_LEDGER.md}}.
\tf{} is that missing implementation, together with a benchmark (\tb{}) and an
evaluation protocol.

\paragraph{Five levels, two targets.} We distinguish
(i)~\emph{proof search} (derive a fixed claim);
(ii)~\emph{conjecture generation} (propose statements);
(iii)~\emph{program synthesis} (evaluator-optimized executable objects);
(iv)~\emph{theory formation} (a \emph{network} of definitions, claims,
transformations, and explanatory structure); and
(v)~\emph{research-program formation} (deciding which families of questions and
representations deserve sustained effort). \tf{} targets (iv) and (v), using
(i)--(iii) as components. This is the line separating it from proof-only agents
such as QED~\cite{QED26}, RMA~\cite{RMA26}, Prover Agent~\cite{ProverAgent25},
and LEAP~\cite{LEAP26}, and from program-search discovery such as
FunSearch~\cite{RomeraParedes24} and AlphaEvolve~\cite{AlphaEvolve25}, which
optimize an executable against a fixed evaluator rather than forming a theory.

\paragraph{Contributions (of this checkpoint).}
\begin{enumerate}
\item A precise \emph{theory-formation task} over a frozen, content-addressed
corpus, with a typed output ontology of Mathematical Cells
(\S\ref{sec:task}).
\item A library of \textbf{thirteen discovery operators} with a common typed
interface --- input types, preconditions, transform, output state, validators,
failure modes, and cost accounting (\S\ref{sec:operators}, App.~\ref{app:ops}).
\item An \textbf{authority-separated architecture} that reuses \mirador{},
\pc{}, and a \cb{}-compatible reviewer, and in which \tf{} structurally cannot
promote (\S\ref{sec:arch}).
\item \tb{}, a \textbf{four-tier evaluation ladder} and task ontology, and a
preregistered baseline/ablation protocol (\S\S\ref{sec:theorybench}--\ref{sec:protocol}).
\item A \textbf{five-stage novelty--verification gauntlet} and a multidimensional
\emph{Verified Knowledge Gain} record (\S\ref{sec:novelty}).
\item A \textbf{frozen, hash-pinned case study} of Bang's affine plank
conjecture, separating rediscovery targets from genuinely open targets
(\S\ref{sec:bang}, App.~\ref{app:freeze}).
\end{enumerate}
Empirical evaluation (\S\ref{sec:results}) is deferred until the protocol is
frozen; this paper does not report it.

\section{From Theorem Proving to Theory Formation}\label{sec:fromproving}

Automated mathematics has a long spine: the Logic Theory
Machine~\cite{NewellSimon57}, proof planning~\cite{Bundy88}, the Robbins
problem~\cite{McCune97}, and large formal verifications~\cite{AppelHaken77,
Gonthier08, Hales17, Heule16}. Theory \emph{formation} --- inventing concepts,
conjecturing, and finding counterexamples --- was pioneered by AM~\cite{Lenat77}
and systematized in HR~\cite{Colton02}, and remains an active target for the
selection problem of ``interestingness''~\cite{Interestingness25}. What is new
is the confluence of (a)~fluent large-model generation, (b)~a governed,
content-addressed knowledge substrate with deterministic
invalidation~\cite{lucius2026mirador}, and (c)~an explicit separation between
proposing and promoting~\cite{lucius2026engine}.

The methodological claim of \tf{} is that \emph{explicit discovery operators},
under a counterexample-first policy and a fixed information boundary, outperform
unconstrained ``think harder'' prompting on theory-formation tasks --- a claim
this paper makes \emph{testable} (\S\ref{sec:protocol}) rather than asserts.

\section{Task Definition and Output Ontology}\label{sec:task}

\begin{task}[Theory formation]\label{task:tf}
Given
(a)~a \emph{governed corpus} $\mathcal{C}@v$ --- a set of Mathematical Cells at a
frozen content-addressed version $v$;
(b)~a \emph{frontier} $\mathcal{F}\subseteq\mathcal{C}@v$ --- open questions and
their dependency neighbourhood;
(c)~a \emph{campaign constitution} $K$ --- exact conventions, admissible
dependencies, per-claim-class certificate standards, novelty/attribution policy,
and stopping conditions; and
(d)~a \emph{budget} $B$ split across six reserves (exploit, orthogonal explore,
counterexample, formalize, barrier, reproduce),
produce a set of typed Mathematical Cells, each created only in state
\state{DRAFT}, \state{OPEN}, or \state{EVIDENCE}, each carrying full genealogy
and a fixed information boundary, maximizing \emph{Verified Knowledge Gain}
(\S\ref{sec:novelty}) per unit cost --- where ``verified/novel'' is decided by
independent downstream machinery, never by \tf{}.
\end{task}

\paragraph{Output ontology.} Outputs are \mirador{} Mathematical
Cells~\cite{lucius2026mirador}: a canonical claim (IR statement plus its
identity-bearing context $\Gamma$) bound to dependencies, transformations,
artifacts, provenance, reviews, and an \emph{epistemic state}. Two commitments
are inherited and load-bearing~\ev{evidenced: \mirador{}
\texttt{schema/mathematical-cell.schema.json}}:
\emph{object type} (one of twenty: \otype{Definition}, \otype{Conjecture},
\otype{Reduction}, \otype{Counterexample}, \otype{Barrier}, \ldots) is
\emph{orthogonal} to \emph{epistemic state} (one of eleven:
\state{DRAFT}$\to$\state{OPEN}$\to$\state{EVIDENCE}$\to$\state{CANDIDATE}$\to$%
\state{FORMALIZED}$\to$\state{AUDITED}$\to$\state{PROMOTED}, plus
\state{REFUTED}, \state{NO\_GO}, \state{SUSPECT}, \state{RETIRED}); and
\emph{claim identity is the SHA-256 of the statement and its context}, never the
prose surface, epistemic state, or artifacts. A cell that is merely reworded has
the same identity; a renamed ``discovery'' therefore collides and is not novel
(\S\ref{sec:novelty}). \tf{} emits cells no higher than \state{EVIDENCE}.

\section{Discovery Operators}\label{sec:operators}

Each operator implements a common typed interface (App.~\ref{app:ops};
schema \texttt{schema/operator.schema.json}):
\[
\text{\op{name}},\ \text{\op{input\_types}},\ \text{\op{preconditions}},\
\text{\op{transform}},\ \text{\op{output\_state}},\ \text{\op{validators}},\
\text{\op{failure\_modes}},\ \text{\op{cost}}.
\]
Every emitted candidate records its genealogy --- operator, parents,
Evidence-Bundle hash, model and prompt versions, and seed --- and is serialized
through \mirador{} so that identity is its \texttt{content\_hash}. Output state
is capped at \state{EVIDENCE}. Numeric witnesses are re-checked in \emph{exact}
arithmetic: a numeric grid is never a proof.

Table~\ref{tab:ops} summarizes the thirteen operators; four representative full
specifications appear in Appendix~\ref{app:ops}. Two operators
(\op{TRANSFER}, \op{CHANGE\_REPRESENTATION}) depend on a cross-domain analogy
retrieval that \pc{} does not yet expose (a v1 gap; \S\ref{sec:limits}, B2).

\begin{tabular}{@{}p{3.1cm}p{9.2cm}@{}}
\toprule
Operator & Purpose (output object $\to$ max state)\\
\midrule
\op{SPECIALIZE} & Restrict parameters/dimension/symmetry to expose tractable structure ($\to$\state{OPEN}).\\
\op{GENERALIZE} & Identify which assumptions a proof actually uses; propose a broader object.\\
\op{COUNTEREXAMPLE\_FIRST} & Attack a claim before allocating proof effort ($\to$\state{EVIDENCE}).\\

\section{TheoryForge Architecture}\label{sec:arch}

\tf{} runs a closed but authority-separated loop: constitution $\to$ \pc{}
Evidence Bundle $\to$ frontier analysis $\to$ operator selection $\to$ candidate
generation $\to$ exact experiments and counterexample search $\to$ formalization
or certificate extraction $\to$ \emph{independent Congress} $\to$ promoted result
or structured failure $\to$ updated corpus and scheduler priors.
The Congress step is the one-way membrane: every step before it may only
\emph{propose};
only the Congress promotes. Because \tf{} writes through
\mirador{}~\cite{lucius2026mirador}, whose transition table forbids an author
from promoting its own cell and requires \texttt{actor=congress} with
independence level $\ge 3$, the ``no promotion authority'' property is
structural~\ev{evidenced: \mirador{} \texttt{transitions.check\_transition}}.

\begin{contract}[Reused substrates]\label{con:substrates}
\mirador{} provides the cell IR, content-addressed identity, dependency graph,
event log, and deterministic invalidation~\cite{lucius2026mirador}. \pc{}
provides dependency-aware retrieval into a hashed Evidence
Bundle~\cite{lucius2026proofcontext} via \texttt{ProofContext.query(nl).bundle}.
Independent review conforms to the \cb{} \texttt{Reviewer} interface and
\texttt{review.schema.json}~\cite{lucius2026congressbench}. \tf{} depends on
these interfaces, not their internals.
\end{contract}

\paragraph{Modules.} frontier mapper; operator policy; candidate generator;
symbolic/computational laboratory (exact arithmetic); counterexample engine;
proof planner; \mirador{} serializer; \pc{} client; formal-backend adapter (see
B1); \cb{}-compatible reviewer interface; research-program scheduler
(\S\ref{sec:scheduler}); and an immutable experiment/event logger.

%================================================================
\section{Research-Program Scheduling}\label{sec:scheduler}

\tf{} manages a \emph{portfolio}, not a single chain of thought. Each program
tracks: hypothesis, representation, target claims, known barriers, expected
information gain, estimated cost, verification debt, novelty risk, dependencies,
failed attempts, retreat conditions, and minimum publishable outcome. Explicit
budgets are reserved for exploitation, orthogonal exploration, counterexample
search, formalization, barriers, and reproduction. The scheduler score (a bandit
/ tree-search heuristic in the tradition of~\cite{Auer02, Browne12}) allocates
resources; \textbf{it is not evidence of mathematical truth}, and if a scalar is
used its weights are preregistered (\S\ref{sec:novelty}).

\section{TheoryBench}\label{sec:theorybench}

\begin{remark}[name]
A name-availability check found no exact ``\tb{}'' but a crowded neighbourhood
(MathBench, UGMathBench, TPBench, GTBench, LemmaBench)~\ev{evidenced: web check
2026-07}. The name is provisional pending a less collision-prone choice.
\end{remark}

\tb{} spans four evaluation worlds. \textbf{Tier A --- synthetic worlds}: small
formal universes with hidden rules, concepts, and invariants; exact ground truth
and full contamination control (the primary quantitative tier). \textbf{Tier B
--- retrospective}: time-slice a corpus before a known
definition/lemma/bridge appeared and ask the system to reconstruct it;
pretrained contamination is treated as a first-order threat and never called
contamination-free. \textbf{Tier C --- expert-authored unseen}: external
mathematicians supply withheld tasks and criteria. \textbf{Tier D --- open
frontier}: bounded open subproblems under external expert governance, where
success includes a new lemma, counterexample, reduction, barrier, or useful
definition, not only full resolution.

Task families: definition synthesis, invariant discovery, conjecture ranking,
counterexample generation, minimal-missing-lemma extraction, representation
change, cross-domain transfer, extremal-family recovery, stability conjecturing,
barrier discovery, research-program selection. Each task manifest fixes:
allowed corpus $@v$, allowed tools, hidden ground truth (where applicable),
discovery budget, evaluation artifacts, novelty boundary, formal or expert
checker, partial-credit rubric, and contamination risk.

\section{Bang Affine Plank Case Study}\label{sec:bang}

\paragraph{The problem.} A \emph{plank} of width $w$ is the region between two
parallel hyperplanes at distance $w$; for a convex body $K$ and a plank $P$ with
normal $u$, the relative width is $\mathrm{rw}_K(P)=\mathrm{width}(P)/
\mathrm{width}_K(u)$. \emph{Bang's affine plank conjecture}
(1951)~\cite{Bang51}: if $K$ is covered by planks $P_1,\dots,P_n$, then
$\sum_i \mathrm{rw}_K(P_i)\ge 1$. Ball proved the centrally symmetric
case~\cite{Ball91}; the general case is open since 1951, open already in the
plane, already for three planks~\cite{BY26}, with best general lower bound
$2/(1+\sqrt d)$~\cite{BY26}. Gardner posed the relative-width witness-measure
existence question~\cite{Gardner88}.

\paragraph{The frozen campaign.} The integrated case study freezes a
submission-ready campaign on the triangle~\cite{Lucius26}
(App.~\ref{app:freeze}). Its \emph{proved} results --- our \textbf{rediscovery
targets} --- include, all with exact-arithmetic backing~\ev{evidenced:
\texttt{drafts/affine-plank-triangle.tex}}:
a transport-defect framework ($D_K(u)$, the least uniform-marginal bound) giving
$\sum\mathrm{rw}\ge 1/D$; a \emph{median theorem with full rigidity} (three
median planks give $\sum\ge1$, equality iff each is the central third); a
\emph{weighted-perimeter witness} (edge masses $1-2p_j$); tightness and rigidity
of the cyclic family; a \emph{three-direction characterization} (a
uniform-marginal witness exists iff the three mid-level lines concur, settling
Gardner's question for three directions on the triangle~\cite{Gardner88}); a
\emph{normalizer obstruction} (Bang's chord-lemma step is sharp, so that
argument cannot reach any normalizer beyond the longest chord); and one
\emph{computer-assisted} result, $C^{111}_\Delta(\tau_0)=1$ at the tilted triple
$\tau_0=(13/25,\tfrac12,\tfrac12)$, discharged by an exact-rational emptiness
certificate ($812{,}651$ leaves) re-verified by an independent
checker~\ev{evidenced: \texttt{c3\_sandwich\_certificate.txt},
\texttt{bb\_certificate\_check.py}}.

\paragraph{Rediscovery vs.\ novelty.} The \textbf{novelty targets} are the
genuinely open subproblems: the quantitative three-plank constant for
non-concurrent, non-facet triples ($C_\Delta(u)=1$?); the flagship
\emph{thin-plank lemma} (which would lift the finite branch-and-bound to the
whole cyclic family); sufficiency of concurrence for $d{+}1$ directions on
$\Delta^{d\ge3}$; and the exact value of $\sup_u D_\Delta(u)\in[3/2,2]$. Any
allegedly novel Bang output must pass the five-stage gauntlet of
\S\ref{sec:novelty}.

\begin{remark}[a naming discrepancy, reported not resolved]
The project brief lists a ``\texttt{2F+1M}'' structure to reconstruct. No such
object exists in the frozen corpus under any spelling~\ev{evidenced: corpus
grep}; the closest are a ``three facets $+$ one plank'' theorem and a
``3 facet planks $+$ 1 tilted'' note. We do \emph{not} invent a
\texttt{2F+1M} theorem; the intended target requires author clarification
(\S\ref{sec:limits}, B4).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[contamination, stated plainly]
The Bang manuscript is public (repository and journal submission)~\ev{evidenced}.
Tier-B rediscovery on it therefore \emph{cannot} be reported as
contamination-free; rediscovered results are labeled rediscovery, never novelty.
The corpus is frozen \emph{with} poison flags on an unretracted-but-unsound note
and on early refuted drafts, so that a run which ``confirms'' them scores as a
failure (App.~\ref{app:freeze}).
\end{remark}

\section{Novelty, Verification and Promotion}\label{sec:novelty}

\paragraph{The five-stage gauntlet (counterexample-first).}
(1)~\pc{} \emph{collision search} --- \texttt{content\_hash} plus
\texttt{classify\_identity} and \texttt{probable\_similarity} over
$\mathcal{C}@v$, and a literature audit for global novelty (must be built; the
primitives exist in \mirador{} but \pc{} exposes no collision client, B2).
(2)~\emph{adversarial counterexample search} \emph{before} any expensive proof
effort.
(3)~\emph{formal / certificate verification where feasible} --- default tier:
exact-rational/symbolic certificate $+$ independent checker (the Bang standard,
where the checker, not the search, is the authority); a proof assistant (Lean~4)
is the named but not-yet-installed backend (B1, B6).
(4)~\emph{fresh-context Congress} --- a \cb{}-conforming reviewer at condition
$\ge$ C4 run by $\ge 2$ model families; the ACCEPT decision is the veto
adjudicator's, never \tf{}'s.
(5)~\emph{external expert review}, identified, not simulated.

\paragraph{Verified Knowledge Gain.} A multidimensional record --- promoted
claims; refuted conjectures with reusable counterexamples; certified barriers;
dependency edges added/removed; theory compression; external novelty status;
cost and verification debt --- \emph{not} an opaque scalar. Quality axes
(correctness, novelty, utility, compression, robustness, efficiency) are measured
separately; ``globally new'' and ``new-to-system'' are distinct labels and never
merged.

\section{Threats to Validity}\label{sec:threats}

We address, and design against: memorized discoveries; superficial renaming as
novelty (caught by content-hash identity); false analogies; problem
modification; reward hacking; evaluator overfitting; benchmark contamination;
model monoculture (mitigated by $\ge 2$ reviewer families); invalid
transformation edges; computational evidence promoted as proof (blocked by the
certificate/checker standard); formalization of a weaker claim (tracked by
\texttt{correspondence\_status}); publication-priority errors; endless
speculative branching and premature convergence (scheduler retreat conditions);
excessive verification debt; and human-expert selection bias. The acute,
project-specific threat is Bang contamination (\S\ref{sec:bang}).

\section{Limitations and Research Agenda}\label{sec:limits}

This is a checkpoint: the architecture and protocol are specified, not executed.
Eight decisions block the freeze and are stated for the record (recommendations
given, not decided here):
\begin{description}
\item[B1 Formal backend.] Lean~4 is named but not installed~\ev{evidenced: \cb{}
B6}. Recommend adopting the exact-rational certificate $+$ checker as the default
formal tier; Lean optional/gated.
\item[B2 \pc{} gaps.] No cross-domain analogy retrieval and no collision-search
client~\ev{evidenced}; blocks \op{TRANSFER}/\op{CHANGE\_REPRESENTATION} and
gauntlet stage~1. Recommend building a thin collision wrapper over \mirador{};
descope cross-domain analogy to a v1 limitation.
\item[B3 Bang contamination.] Public manuscript; keep Bang as an illustrative
case with rediscovery labeled as such, and make Tier-A synthetic worlds the
primary quantitative evidence.
\item[B4 \texttt{2F+1M} target.] No such object exists; needs PI clarification.
\item[B5 Budget \& model families.] Reviewer independence needs $\ge 2$ families
at real dollar cost.
\item[B6 Frontier target \& expert.] Which open subproblem, and \emph{who} is the
identified external expert (manuscript authors? outreach thread?).
\item[B7 Congress composition.] \texttt{StubReviewer} is not evidence; a real
evaluative Congress needs LLM reviewers plus a human expert.
\item[B8 VKG scalar.] Multidimensional for reporting; a preregistered scalar only
for scheduler allocation.
\end{description}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

\tf{} operationalizes the distinction between emitting statements and forming
theory, as the propose-only Discovery Plane of a governed engine. This checkpoint
delivers the task definition, operator library, architecture, benchmark design,
and verification protocol, plus a frozen case-study corpus --- and, deliberately,
\emph{no discovery claims}. The next step is to resolve B1--B8, freeze the
protocol and manifests, and run the preregistered evaluation, reporting Verified
Knowledge Gain (including null results) that regenerates from raw artifacts.

\section{Discovery-operator specifications (excerpt)}\label{app:ops}
Four representative full specifications; the remaining nine follow the same
seven-field schema in \texttt{operators/OPERATOR\_API.md}.

\begin{description}
\item[\op{COUNTEREXAMPLE\_FIRST}] \emph{in}: \otype{Conjecture}/\otype{Statement}.
\emph{pre}: falsifiable, with a searchable model class. \emph{transform}: compile
the negation to a search \emph{before} any proof budget. \emph{out}:
\otype{Counterexample} (\state{EVIDENCE}) or a survived-search annotation.
\emph{val}: exact-arithmetic re-check of the witness. \emph{fail}: search too
large $\to$ bounded-search barrier note. \emph{cost}: search wall-clock $+$ calls.
\item[\op{SYNTHESIZE\_DEFINITION}] \emph{in}: $\ge 2$
\otype{Example}/\otype{Experiment}/\otype{Theorem} sharing structure.
\emph{pre}: a retrievable repeated pattern. \emph{transform}: propose a concept
compressing it; test statement/proof/retrieval improvement. \emph{out}:
\otype{Definition} (\state{DRAFT}). \emph{val}: IR type-check $+$ non-collision.
\emph{fail}: pure renaming $\to$ flagged non-novel. \emph{cost}: generation $+$
compression measurement.
\item[\op{MINIMAL\_MISSING\_LEMMA}] \emph{in}: gapped \otype{Proof} blueprint.
\emph{pre}: localized gap. \emph{transform}: infer the weakest closing
obligation. \emph{out}: \otype{Lemma} (\state{OPEN}) $+$ \texttt{requires} edge.
\emph{val}: remaining steps type-check under the lemma. \emph{fail}: lemma
$\equiv$ theorem $\to$ flagged. \emph{cost}: planner calls. (Bang: the thin-plank
lemma.)
\item[\op{BARRIER\_SEARCH}] \emph{in}: method family $+$ target bound. \emph{pre}:
method formalizable as a bounded transformation. \emph{transform}: prove it
cannot cross an obstruction. \emph{out}: \otype{Barrier}/\state{NO\_GO}
(\state{EVIDENCE}). \emph{val}: exact/certified obstruction. \emph{fail}:
heuristic only $\to$ \state{EVIDENCE}, not \state{NO\_GO}. (Bang: the normalizer
obstruction.)
\end{description}
